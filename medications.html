Health & Safety for Blood Donors

Maintaining good health and following safe practices before and after donating blood ensures your safety and helps patients receive the best care.

Stay Hydrated

Drink plenty of water before and after donation to prevent dizziness and maintain blood volume.

Eat Iron-rich Foods

Include leafy greens, red meat, and beans to boost hemoglobin levels before donation.

Get Adequate Sleep

Rest well the night before donation to feel energized and reduce fatigue.

Avoid Alcohol

Do not drink alcohol 24 hours before donating as it can affect blood quality.

Light Exercise

Stay active, but avoid heavy workouts immediately before donation.

Take Recommended Medications

Consult your doctor for any medications; avoid certain blood thinners before donation.

Avoid Fatty Foods

High-fat foods can affect blood tests; eat balanced meals before donating.

Check Blood Pressure

Ensure your blood pressure is in normal range before donating for safety.

Maintain Hygiene

Wash hands and follow basic hygiene to prevent infections during donation.

Healthy Snacks After Donation

Eat light snacks like fruits, nuts, or juice to restore energy post-donation.

Monitor Side Effects

Observe for dizziness or fatigue and rest if necessary. Report unusual reactions.

Community Awareness

Share healthy practices and encourage friends and family to donate safely.
